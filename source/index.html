What do you keep in the cloud?

Your private data should stay private. But if you keep it in the cloud, today, it isn't. Because "the cloud" is some computers in a data center somewhere. Some company owns those computers. And that company has employees with access to the computers.

Hopefully the company has the ethics to keep your data safe and secure. Ethics was never the main characteristic that you would associate with companies, but they have a privacy policy. And hopefully none of the employees with access to your data would abuse their position.

Unfortunately, it's not entirely up to the company and their employees. As recent events have shown, government officials can demand access to any data that anyone else is storing for you. And they can do it in secret, so you don't even know.

Fog Cloud is a project to make sure that if you keep your data in the cloud, nobody else can read it unless you explicitly share it with them.

Your Address Book

It's amazing: When you get a new phone all of your contacts are already there. And the same contact list is shared with the webmail service you use. You probably don't have a paper address book anymore, instead the complete list of everyone you know how to get in touch with is stored electronically.

Taking somebody's address book was always a classic surveillence move. There's no reason to let anyone do it without a warrant.

Your Email

No charge webmail has become ubiquitous. Not only does it let you read your email in any web browser or on any mobile device, it stores an archive of all the mail you've sent or recieved pretty much forever.

Different people use email in different ways, but for many it's one of their primary forms of communication and organization, especially professionally. Historically, the privacy of postal messages has been one of the most obvious requirements of private communication, and this applies no less strongly to email.

Your Instant Messages and Text Messages

Like email, these are written personal communication and should remain private both when sent and when archived.

Your Files

Your files are available on any computer you sit down at, automatically. Whether this is your masters thesis, your new business plan, or your Bitcoin wallet, there's no reason anyone should have access to your private files unless you want to share them.

Your Calendar

You want your phone to remind you that you have an appointment with the doctor tomorrow, but when you go to the doctor is none of anyone else's business.

Your Browser History and Bookmarks

Are you now or have you ever been a member of the Communist party? Have you read the Communist Manifesto? The right to read privately is as core to a free society as anything can be.

What is Fog Cloud?

You should be able to have access to all your data and communications across all of your devices without giving access to anyone else.

Fog Cloud is a project to develop the software to make that at least a practical option.
